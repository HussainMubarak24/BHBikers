There are many names for bikes in the market these days, but mainly there are only 3 types we would to focus on.

 

Choosing the bike is mainly related to it's own purpose. What exactly you want the bike for? Do you want to have a bike for cruising around the city? or you are in love with adventures and discovering new roads? Perhaps the speed is what attracts you?

 

What ever the reason of the choosing the bike, you must focus on the reason of buying new one. Cutting the chase, there are 3 type as we mentioned, Cruiser bike, Mountain bike and Race bike.

 

Cruise Bikes

 

Cruiser bikes are famous casual riding bikes which possess the following unique features:

 

Extra wide tires

Upright handlebar

Simple design

Large and comfortable seats

Coaster brake – backpedaling can stop the bike

 

The parts used to make cruiser bikes are machined from light and strong materials such as steel, aluminum, cast plastic, alloys, and rubber.

Mountain Bikes

 

In thinking about the type of cycling that you want to do, if you plan to ride "off road" a lot, like using your bike to traverse the farms or zip through the desert on sandy or rocky trails, a mountain bike is probably what you want. Mountain bikes are designed for riding under more rugged conditions, and typically:

 

Have a stouter, more upright frame

Offer higher clearance to get over rocks, logs and through ruts, etc.

Can take a lot of stress and abuse and still allow the rider to comfortably negotiate rugged terrain and go over or through obstacles that he or she may encounter on the trail.

 

Simply a bike for every road and every where..!

Race Bikes

 

We’re talking here about bikes intended for racing. That means low handlebars, long top tube, and a flat-back, stretched position. Combined with the typical geometry of a race bike — 73-74° seat and head angles and short chain-stays  these are bikes that feel quick, respond quickly but predictably to steering, but are still comfortable all day, as long as you’re flexible enough to handle the position.

 

Race bikes have either very light light wheels, or, even better, aero deep-section rims. Light wheels add a bit of speed on hills, just by lightening the whole bike, but aero wheels add speed everywhere, which far outweighs the disadvantage of their extra weight.

 

If you intended to be a racer, then this is your answer!

These are important safety tips you must consider before heading the road:

 

 

• Always follow the highway road instructions, if you see Bicycle Sign then this road is designed to be used by bicyclists.

 

• Don’t jump red lights and don’t cycle on the pavement unless it’s a designated cycle path.

 

• Wear a helmet, and if you cycling in the night then make sure to use lights to be seen.

 

• Make sure motorists can see you, and if you come up on road that need to take changing lanes, then make sure that drivers know by giving them hand-waves and eye contact.

 

• Use your bell – not all pedestrians can see you.

 

• Don’t assume the vehicle is going straight ahead just because it isn’t signaling right. Always avoid ‘undertaking’ any vehicle in this situation – it’s better to hang back until the vehicle has moved off.
